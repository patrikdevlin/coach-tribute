Lilly

"Just put it under John” is coach’s favorite catchphrase when he takes his grandkids out to Jamie Hollanders for breakfast. Well maybe second favorite catchphrase behind “special squeeze”, “golden handshake” and “ I can’t wait until you go to Lafayette”. Coach loves Lafayette and pours tons of effort into his annual pig roast and religiously follows all their sports. He follows not only the Lafayette Leopards but also the New Hope-Solebury lions. While playing in my field hockey and lacrosse games I can hear Coach’s legendary throat clear from any point on the field. I’m never surprised when I walk off the pitch and see Coach in a soup-stained sweater or the vest cousin John bought him for Christmas. Speaking of Christmas, Coach is the oyster stew master. His secret in the recipe is using a homemade shoe horn that I made him as a stirrer. It may not be sanitary but it sure enhances the flavor. Coach not only gives great food to people; he also brings great food to dogs. Whenever he comes over, he usually brings a pig heart, pig ears, pig liver, or pizza slices for Chance the dog. When he doesn’t bring food, he still finds snacks in our cabinet to feed Chance even though I beg him not to. The best part about Coach is that he is the only good opponent in gin. We love that game and have played it together ever since he finished teaching me how to swim. All in all, Coach is the best grandfather anyone could ask for and we all love him dearly. HAPPY BIRTHDAY COACH!! I hope your birthday is as great as some real authentic maple syrup.

Love,

Your Favorite Grandkid (Lilly)

Collin

When my friends find out that I call my grandfather “Coach,” it usually requires a bit of explanation. It’s not a common thing to call a grandparent, but to me it’s always felt completely appropriate. Throughout my life, Coach has fulfilled the spirit of his moniker in a very real way, whether it was showing me how to fish on the shores of the Outer Banks, taking me to eat the best peperoni in the world at Conte’s Pizza, or teaching me important life lessons like, “Restaurants won’t always have real maple syrup, so don’t be afraid to bring your own.” When he eventually became my actual “coach” by taking over the as the lawyer advisor for my Mock Trial team in high school, it was a natural role for him to assume. And I know I’m not an exception. Coach has been a constant presence in the lives of all of my siblings and cousins, guiding us figuratively and, quite often, literally – like how he would guide us to the Duck Deli each year as though we were actually a flock of ducklings following single-file behind our parent. I’m sure Coach shared plenty of words of wisdom on those trips to Conte’s or the Duck Deli, but it’s the trips themselves that I remember most because they became important traditions for the family, the memories of which are as valuable and long-lasting as the lessons Coach imparted along the way.

Claire

I can’t say enough about what an amazing grandfather Coach is or his incredible influence on my life. When considering his influence, the thing that stands out is his desire to share his interests with his grandchildren. Be it sailing on the catamaran, shad fishing or the Lafayette Pig Roast, he’s given us all a glimpse of how to live a life full of passion.

However, the thing about coach that has had the greatest impact on me is actually something that he lacks: a filter. He’s passed this down to my father and with their collective running commentary, I’ve developed a thick skin, which has served me well throughout my life. The examples are countless, but I’ll share one that has etched a place in my memory... Being the extremely present grandparents that they are, Coach and Grandy always attended pre-dance photo shoots throughout middle school and high school. On the eve of my eighth grade dance, they were at our house along with several of my friends as we got ready for our last dance of middle school. It should come as no surprise that at that point in my life I had only spoken with the opposite sex when required by teachers and wouldn’t have known what to do if one had approached me to dance. I spent hours searching for the right dress and finally found the perfect one. It was beautiful, classy and clearly sent the message: back off. After an hour or so of hair, makeup and gossip it was time for the dress unveiling. As I walked onto the back deck, Coach was of course the first to comment: “Oh my God... she’s got breasts!” You can imagine how this may have impacted a normal pre-pubescent, self-conscious girl. Not me. I paused for a moment, looked down and replied proudly “Sure do!” (For those who are confused, I was wearing a push-up bra and they stopped growing shortly thereafter. No one has ever uttered those words to me since.)

Coach made me value honesty and directness in communication, which has helped me immensely in navigating social and work situations. I’m less sensitive, I try not to read into things, and instead look for the positive spin. It saves lots of unnecessary worry about what someone actually meant. Coach helps us all live in a world where people say what they mean and mean what they say.

As I left for the dance that evening, I had a renewed confidence knowing that if any male approached, I could knock him out with my gigantic breasts.
